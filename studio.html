GIDE — Studio

Engineering clarity.
Material honesty.

GIDE is an industrial design studio focused on practical, well-made objects. The work sits at the intersection of engineering discipline and warm-minimal form — each piece designed to function cleanly, age well, and feel at home in everyday use.

History

One object at a time.

GIDE was founded in 2025 with a straightforward goal: make useful objects with clear reasoning behind every decision. A background in mechanical engineering shapes how each piece is developed, tested, and refined.

The pace is deliberate. Releasing one object at a time allows the work to move through prototyping, tolerance checks, and material evaluation without compromise. Sharing the documentation behind the process gives you confidence in what you're buying.

2025

GIDE founded. Studio established with a focus on industrial design for the intentional collector.

Core Principles

01

Traceable Design

Every decision traces to a functional requirement. If it doesn't serve a purpose, it gets removed.

02

Material Truth

Materials are selected for mechanical properties first. The aesthetic follows from the material's nature — not the other way around.

03

Deliberate Pace

One object at a time. Every detail meets engineering standards before anything ships.

04

Open Documentation

Case studies, tolerances, and process notes are published alongside the work. Understanding what you own matters.

05

Digital Parity

Tools are built to the same standard as the objects — minimal, purposeful, documented. Free to use because good utility shouldn't have a gate.

06

Community First

The people building alongside GIDE shape what gets made next. Suggestions, modifications, and documentation from the community are welcome.
